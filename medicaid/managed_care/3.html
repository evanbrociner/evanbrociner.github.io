Section 2: Strategies for Targeted and Effective Care Management

  • Targeted Interventions for High-Cost Subgroups: Identifying and providing tailored medical and non-medical services to high-cost subgroups, such as seniors and people with disabilities, can lead to significant cost savings. (Dispelling The Myths Hindering Medicaid Innovation)
  • Tailored Interventions for Super-Utilizers: While a small percentage of patients account for a large proportion of healthcare costs, their needs often fluctuate. Improved predictive models and personalized interventions can help manage their care more effectively. (For Many Patients Who Use Large Amounts Of Health Care Services, The Need Is Intense Yet Temporary)
  • Comprehensive Support for High-Need, High-Cost Patients: Programs like the one in Memphis, Tennessee, demonstrate that frequent follow-ups, addressing social needs, and integrated care can significantly reduce healthcare costs for this population. (How to Improve Care for High-Need, High-Cost Medicaid Patients)
