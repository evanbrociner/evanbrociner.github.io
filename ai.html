AI 101

Term Description
Neural Network A type of machine learning algorithm inspired by the structure of the human brain, composed of interconnected "neurons" that process and transmit information. This is the base technology that allows for most modern AI work.
Natural Language Processing (NLP) The field of AI concerned with enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language. Used in healthcare for analyzing medical notes or providing conversational AI interfaces.
Pre-Trained A machine learning model that has already been trained on a large, general-purpose dataset, enabling it to have a foundational understanding of patterns and can be adapted for more specific tasks.
Fine-Tuning A process where a pre-trained machine learning model is further trained on a smaller, more specialized dataset to improve its performance on a specific task.

What My 90-Year-Old Mom Taught Me About The Future Of AI In Health Care

A very well written article by Zak Kohane.

Key Points
  • Zak's mother's heart condition led to frequent hospitalizations due to fluid buildup, highlighting the limitations of traditional outpatient monitoring.
  • Zak implemented a simple algorithm involving daily weighing and medication adjustments, enabled by a connected scale that allowed remote monitoring.
  • This approach successfully prevented further hospitalizations, demonstrating the power of combining technology with personalized care strategies.
  • While AI shows promise in healthcare, it cannot fully replace the human element – clinical judgment, persuasive skills, and understanding of the full spectrum of patient needs remain crucial.

Who Pays for Healthcare AI? (Part I)

An article by Morgan Cheatman. Current payment models are hindering healthcare AI adoption – we need business and pricing model innovation to realize our potential as an industry.

Key Points
  • Healthcare providers have limited IT budgets, making it difficult to adopt expensive new AI technologies.
  • Existing payment models in healthcare aren't designed to support the unique value proposition of AI solutions.
  • The healthcare AI industry needs innovative business and pricing models, potentially including usage-based and performance-based approaches, to better capture the value these solutions can provide.
Six imperatives for AI-first companies

Another article by Morgan Cheatman. What it takes to build generational AI companies in healthcare, life sciences, and beyond.

Six imperatives for AI-first companies
  • Create and sustain an undeniable data advantage.
  • Recruit and empower AI scientists.
  • Support a flexible AI stack.
  • Establish distribution moats.
  • Center safety and ethics in model development.
  • Earn trust by solving real problems.
AI has entered the chat

A collaboration with XPC, Ben Lee from Quite a Few Claims, who spearheaded the content and analysis of strategic plays in the world of clinical AI, and Blake Madden from Workweek who reviewed financial and commercial activity in the space.

Key Points
  • Streamlining Processes: AI can automate note-taking, medical record summarization, and patient data analysis.
  • Advancing Opportunities: Advances in algorithms, data access, and regulatory changes are fostering a climate ripe for AI adoption.
  • Prioritizing Improvements: Focus development efforts on areas where AI provides a clear, significant advantage in areas like administrative burdens, personalized care, and complex diagnostics.
Lessons from Pear VC: What is in a good healthcare AI startup?

An article including great figures highlighting some of the real opportunities for AI in healthcare.

Key Points
  • Understand Who Pays: Success hinges on understanding the complex network of incentives and decision-makers within the healthcare system.
  • Specialization is Key: Build specialized AI models tailored to specific healthcare workflows. Consider using proprietary data for a competitive edge.
  • Ethical AI is Essential: Prioritize safety, transparency, and unbiased AI solutions in development and deployment.
  • Big Market, Big Opportunity: Ensure both product-market fit and a sizable target market to support a thriving healthcare business.
